NICOLE ZEFANYA

SINGER-SONGWRITER

ABOUT

Nicole Zefanya, professionally known as NIKI, is an Indonesian singer-songwriter, record producer, and global music sensation. Born on January 24, 1999, in Jakarta, Indonesia, she emerged as a breakout star in the alternative R&B and pop scenes. NIKI began cultivating her musical talents at a young age, inspired by her mother's gospel singing and her own love for storytelling through song. As a teenager, she gained early recognition by posting acoustic covers and original tracks on YouTube, which showcased her soulful voice and lyrical maturity. Her big break came in 2017 when she joined 88rising, a prominent music collective focused on Asian talent, which catapulted her into the global spotlight. Since then, she has released critically acclaimed projects like Zephyr (2018), Moonchild (2020), Nicole (2022), and Buzz (2024). Known for her poetic songwriting, genre-bending sound, and deep emotional resonance, NIKI has built a loyal international fanbase. As of April 2025, she has accumulated over 4.4 billion streams on Spotify, making her the most-streamed Indonesian artist of all time. Her influence extends beyond music—she’s become a symbol of Southeast Asian pride and a trailblazer for Asian women in Western pop culture

CAREER

NIKI began her music career by uploading acoustic covers and original songs to her YouTube channel, @nzee24, during her teenage years, quickly gaining a loyal fanbase. At just 15, she won a competition to open for Taylor Swift in Jakarta during the Red Tour in 2014, a pivotal moment in her early career. In 2017, she joined the American music collective 88rising, which focuses on promoting Asian talent, launching her onto the global stage. Her debut EP, Zephyr (2018), blended R&B and pop, establishing her unique sound. In 2020, she released Moonchild, a concept album about identity and self-discovery, inspired by her move from Jakarta to the U.S. Her second album, Nicole (2022), revisited and reimagined songs she wrote in her youth, offering deeply personal and nostalgic storytelling. In 2024, she released her third album, Buzz, which explored the emotional complexities of adulthood and was accompanied by a global tour across Asia, Australia, Europe, and North America. Among her notable achievements, she became the first Indonesian solo artist toperform on Jimmy Kimmel Live! in 2024.
