Inside Out 2

Inside Out 2 (2024) is the highly anticipated sequel to Pixar’s beloved 2015 film Inside Out. Directed by Kelsey Mann and produced by Mark Nielsen, the film continues to explore the emotional life of Riley Andersen, now a 13-year-old navigating the complexities of adolescence and high school. The original voice cast, including Amy Poehler (Joy), Phyllis Smith (Sadness), Lewis Black (Anger), Diane Lane (Mom), and Kyle MacLachlan (Dad), reprise their roles. They are joined by a talented new cast featuring Maya Hawke, Ayo Edebiri, and others who bring fresh energy and new emotional perspectives to the story. The film’s development began in early 2020, with Mann drawing on her own childhood experiences to create an authentic portrayal of adolescent emotional development.

One notable milestone of Inside Out 2 is that it became the first Pixar feature film scored by a woman, Andrea Datzman, marking a significant step for diversity and representation in the industry. The animation team employed innovative techniques, including isometric mapping, to maintain spatial consistency within Riley’s mind and emotional landscape. Upon its premiere at the El Capitan Theatre in Hollywood on June 10, 2024, and subsequent wide release on June 14, the film was met with critical acclaim for its heartfelt storytelling, engaging characters, and vibrant animation. Commercially, Inside Out 2 was a massive success, grossing approximately $1.7 billion worldwide. It set multiple box-office records, becoming the highest-grossing animated film ever from September 2024 until February 2025, and the highest-grossing film of 2024 overall. It also ranked as the eighth-highest-grossing film of all time at the moment of its release.

Characters

JOY

Joy is one of the five core emotions living inside the mind of a young girl named Riley. She represents happiness, optimism, and positivity. With bright blue hair, a glowing yellow aura, and an energetic personality, Joy is constantly driven to keep Riley happy and help her see the bright side of every situation. As the central emotion, she often takes the lead in managing Riley’s memories and guiding her responses, believing that staying positive is the key to a good life.

SADNESS

Sadness is a gentle and empathetic emotion inside Riley’s mind. Though often seen as a downer, Sadness plays a crucial role by helping Riley process difficult feelings, connect with others, and find comfort. Over time, Sadness shows that experiencing and expressing sadness is essential for emotional healing and growth, proving that all emotions are important for a balanced, healthy life. Sadness’s quiet presence reminds us that it’s okay to slow down and reflect during tough times. Ultimately, Sadness helps Riley—and us—embrace the full spectrum of emotions needed for true healing and happiness.

ANGER

Anger is a fiery and passionate emotion that helps Riley express frustration and stand up for herself. Though quick-tempered, Anger plays an important role by protecting Riley and motivating her to take action when things feel unfair or challenging. He helps Riley recognize when something needs to change and gives her the energy to face those challenges head-on. Despite his fiery nature, Anger is an essential part of Riley’s emotional balance. Through Anger, the film shows that it’s okay to feel upset, as long as those feelings are understood and managed in a healthy way.

DISGUST

Disgust is one of Riley’s core emotions, responsible for protecting her from things that might be unpleasant or harmful. With her green skin, sharp wit, and confident attitude, Disgust helps Riley avoid anything gross, unsafe, or socially embarrassing. She acts as a kind of internal filter, keeping Riley away from bad tastes, smells, and awkward situations. Disgust’s role is important because it helps Riley make smart choices and maintain her comfort and dignity. Her blend of sass and caution adds humor and balance to the group of emotions, showing that protecting oneself from negativity is just as vital as feeling joy or sadness.

ENVY

Envy isn’t one of the main emotions featured in Inside Out, but if we think about it conceptually, envy is a feeling where someone wants what another person has—whether it’s possessions, qualities, or experiences. It often involves feelings of jealousy, comparison, or dissatisfaction. If Envy were an emotion inside Riley’s mind, it might make her feel unhappy or restless when she sees others having things she wishes for, pushing her to want more or feel left out. Unlike Joy or Sadness, Envy would likely highlight the challenges of comparing ourselves to others, reminding us how important it is to focus on gratitude and self-acceptance instead.

FEAR

Fear is one of Riley’s core emotions, responsible for keeping her safe by anticipating potential dangers or problems. With his wide eyes, nervous energy, and cautious nature, Fear is always on high alert, quickly reacting to anything that might threaten Riley’s well-being. He helps Riley avoid risky situations and think carefully before acting.Though often anxious and easily startled, Fear plays a vital role in protecting Riley from harm and helping her prepare for challenges. His cautiousness balances out the more impulsive emotions, reminding Riley to be careful and aware of her surroundings. Fear shows that feeling scared isn’t just natural—it’s essential for staying safe and making smart decisions.

ANXIETY

Anxiety isn’t explicitly personified as a separate emotion in Inside Out, but it’s closely related to the role of Fear. Anxiety involves feelings of worry, nervousness, and unease about uncertain or potentially negative outcomes. If anxiety were a distinct emotion inside Riley’s mind, it would likely amplify Fear’s caution, causing Riley to overthink situations or feel overwhelmed by stress. Anxiety helps alert us to potential problems, but when it becomes too intense, it can interfere with daily life by creating excessive worry or avoidance. In that sense, anxiety highlights the importance of balance in managing emotions—recognizing risks without letting fear take over completely. It reminds us that it’s okay to feel uneasy sometimes, but also important to find ways to stay calm and grounded.

ENNUI

Ennui is a French word that means a feeling of listlessness, boredom, or dissatisfaction arising from a lack of excitement or purpose. It’s a deeper, more existential kind of boredom—more than just being bored for a moment, it’s a sense of weariness or restlessness about life in general. Though Inside Out doesn’t specifically include Ennui as an emotion, if it did, Ennui would represent those moments when Riley feels stuck, unmotivated, or disconnected from what’s happening around her. This emotion would highlight the importance of finding meaning and engagement in life, reminding us that sometimes it’s okay to feel a bit lost or restless as we search for what truly excites and fulfills us.
